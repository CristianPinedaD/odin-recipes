Mofongo

Description

This traditional Puerto Rican (or Dominican) dish is a delicious explosion of flavor. It is so simple to make that I can make it with my eyes closed at this point. Follow this guide step by step to become a Mofongo professional.

DISCLAIMER: Repeated indulgence may result in deliciousness. White people should consult with a health professional before consuming.

Ingredients

  • Green Plantains
  • Garlic
  • Neutral Oil
  • Olive Oil
  • Bacon or Chicharron
  • Limes
  • Chicken Broth
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Peel the garlic and mash it with salt in a Pilon (or mortar if no Pilon)
  2. Fill a pot with the neutral oil on medium heat.
  3. In the meantime, peel plantains and cut into thick round slices.
  4. Once the oil is hot enough to fry the plantains with a soft fizz (not immediately browning them, that is), drop the plantains.
  5. Fry the plantains until they have become golden brown
  6. Once they are ready, strain onto a plate covered in paper towels
  7. In a pan on medium head, fry off bacon or Chicharron, set aside.
  8. Mash plantains in the Pilon, adding mashed garlic to taste.
  9. Once protein is ready, strain and add to mashed plantains in the Pilon.
  10. Serve in one or more piles of Mofongo, with a small bowl of Chicken Broth, and a little lime on the side.
